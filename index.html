An Clásal Coibhneasta

The Relative Clause · Notes, Exercises & Translation

What is the Relative Clause?

A relative clause qualifies a noun — like an adjective, giving extra information. The noun being described is the réamhtheachtaí (antecedent). The connecting word is the mír choibhneasta (relative particle). The key is whether the clause is direct or indirect.

Direct vs Indirect

Direct DÍREACH

Antecedent is the subject or direct object of the verb — nothing is "left over".

English test
No leftover pronoun → DIRECT
✓ "the man who sings" · "the book that I read"
Present / future
antecedent + a + SÉIMHIÚ
an duine a fheicim — the person I see
an bia atá réidh — the food that is ready (tá → atá)
Past — regular
antecedent + a + SÉIMHIÚ
na ceoltóirí a bhain an chéad duais
Ulster/Connacht endings
Present: -(e)as / -(e)anns · Future: -f(e)as
an duine a mholas · an múinteoir a fheicfeas tú · bheas (= relative of beidh)

Indirect INDÍREACH

Antecedent connected to the verb through a preposition or through possession — there is always a leftover pronoun/possessive.

English test
Leftover pronoun → INDIRECT
✓ "the house where I live in it" · "the man whose son is in Australia"
Present / future
antecedent + a + URÚ + leftover
an teach a bhfanaim ann · an bhean a bhfuil a mac san Astráil
Past — regular
antecedent + ar + SÉIMHIÚ + leftover
an teach ar fhan mé ann · na daoine ar bhuaigh a bpáiste an chéad duais
Past — irregular
antecedent + a + URÚ + leftover
an teach a raibh siad ann · an fear a bhfaca Seán a charr

Negative Forms DIÚLTACH

Copula in relative clauses
Direct: is/ba/nár(bh) unchanged. Ba → ab before vowel/fh.
Indirect: ar replaces is: an duine ar leis an carr · an cailín ar mhaith liom labhairt léi

Trigger Words

DIRECT

cad, cé, cathain, cá fhad, cé mhéad, cén uair, conas, cad é mar, nuair

INDIRECT

cén áit / cá háit / an áit, cén chaoi / an chaoi, cén fáth / an fáth, an dóigh / cén dóigh, cad chuige, an tslí / cén tslí, mar (= where)

Cleachtadh 2, Cuid C — Work it out, then reveal the answer.

1 / 10
Where is the money that was on that table?
Direct past — the money was on the table (it = subject). "cá bhfuil" + direct relative.
Cá bhfuil an t-airgead a bhí ar an tábla sin?
Direct, past (bhí irregular — already lenited): a + séimhiú
2 / 10
The professor who gave the speech last night will speak to the class today.
Direct past — the professor gave the speech (subject). Irregular verb tabhair → thug.
Labhróidh an t-ollamh a thug an óráid aréir leis an rang inniu.
Direct, past irregular (thug = past of tabhair): a + séimhiú
3 / 10
The boy to whom I gave the phone message forgot to leave a note.
Indirect prep — "dó" (to him) refers back to the boy. Past, regular verb tabhair → thug.
Rinne an buachaill ar thug mé an teachtaireacht fóin dó dearmad nóta a fhágáil.
Indirect prep, past regular: ar + séimhiú (tug → thug)
4 / 10
That's the mobile phone I want for my birthday.
"I want" = "tá uaim" — the phone is what I want (prep "uaim" = from me/I want). Indirect prep, present.
Sin é an fón póca a bhfuil uaim do mo lá breithe.
Indirect prep, present: a + urú (fuil → bhfuil)
5 / 10
This is the way she prepares her lesson plans.
"an dóigh" triggers an INDIRECT relative clause. Present tense.
Sin é an dóigh a n-ullmhaíonn sí a cuid pleananna ceachta.
Indirect (an dóigh = indirect trigger), present: a + urú (u → n-u)
6 / 10
Which year did they move back to Ireland?
"Cén bhliain" + direct relative. Past, irregular — fill → d'fhill.

Direct future, verb beginning with vowel/f: keep d'/d'fh as-is after "a"
7 / 10
Here's the girl whose mother won first prize in the school draw.
Indirect genitive — "a máthair" (her mother). Past, regular verb buaigh.
Seo í an cailín ar bhuaigh a máthair an chéad duais i gcrannchur na scoile.
Indirect genitive, past regular: ar + séimhiú (buaigh → bhuaigh)
8 / 10

Indirect prep — "léi" (with/to her). Past, regular verb labhair.

Indirect prep, past regular: ar + séimhiú (labhair — l unchanged here, but ar is the key marker)

"rud a" = a thing which — the whole clause is the antecedent. Direct, past regular: a + séimhiú (cuir → chuir)

Indirect genitive negative, present: nach + urú (téann → dtéann)
